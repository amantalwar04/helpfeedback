Reuse Strategy

The Microchip reuse strategy is based on the commonality seen across the Microchip products themselves. With careful planning and conditional processing,Microchip can single-source information between its products. The Microchip Information Architecture relies on several different approaches to support its reuse strategy:
  • Individual maps are compiled for multiple types of output.
  • Maps point to common topics that can be reused in their entirety.
  • Collection files contain common content that can be reused across the enterprise or within a product or version.
  • Conditional processing filters content based on specific data.
  • Variables substitute phrase-level content such as product names.
  • Differences are pushed into common files at runtime.

Use this table to choose between the approaches described in this section.

If the following is true Use this approach
Maps or topics can be used without any changes in another publication Reusing Topics and Maps
You want to reuse portions of another topic within your current topic Reusing Elements Through Collection Files
Unique paragraphs, applicable to only a subset of conditions, represent greater than 50% of the topic content Reusing Elements Through Collection Files
Unique paragraphs, applicable to only a subset of conditions, represent less than 50% of the topic content
Variations in topic content due to a specific condition will always have corresponding content for all other conditions. That is, a version of the content exists under all conditions.
Differences in topic content should be managed by separate PDUs; they cannot be maintained by a single topic.
